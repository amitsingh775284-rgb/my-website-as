Getting There

How to Reach Ayodhya

Ayodhya is well-connected by air, rail, and road. Choose your preferred mode of travel.

✈
✈️

By Air

The Maharishi Valmiki International Airport (Ayodhya Dham) is now operational, connecting Ayodhya directly to major Indian cities.

  • Direct flights from Delhi (1.5 hrs)
  • Direct flights from Mumbai (2.5 hrs)
  • Flights from Ahmedabad & Bengaluru
  • Airport is 8 km from city center
🚂

By Train

Ayodhya Junction (AY) and Ayodhya Cantt (AYCTJ) are the main railway stations with excellent connectivity across India.

  • From Delhi — 10-12 hours
  • From Lucknow — 3-4 hours
  • From Varanasi — 5-6 hours
  • Multiple daily express trains
🚗

By Road

Ayodhya is connected by NH-27 and state highways, with excellent bus services from major UP cities.

  • From Lucknow — 130 km (2.5 hrs)
  • From Varanasi — 200 km (4 hrs)
  • From Gorakhpur — 180 km (3.5 hrs)
  • UPSRTC & private buses available

Plan Your Days

Suggested 3-Day Itinerary

Make the most of your sacred journey with our recommended itinerary.

📋
Day 1 — Arrival & Main Temples

🏛 Begin Your Sacred Journey

Arrive in Ayodhya and check into your hotel. Start with darshan at Shri Ram Janmabhoomi temple (arrive early to avoid crowds). After lunch, visit Hanuman Garhi and climb the 76 steps for panoramic views. End the day witnessing the magical Sarayu Aarti at Ram Ki Paidi ghats.

Day 2 — Heritage & Culture

👑 Explore the Golden Legacy

Begin with Kanak Bhawan — the golden palace. Visit Dashrath Mahal and Sita Ki Rasoi for a glimpse into royal heritage. After lunch, explore Nageshwarnath Temple and Treta Ke Thakur. Visit Tulsi Smarak Bhawan and explore the local markets for Ayodhya's famous pedas and sacred souvenirs.

Day 3 — Ghats & Departure

🌊 Riverside Serenity

Early morning holy dip at Guptar Ghat. Visit Swarg Dwar and Mani Parvat for panoramic valley views. Enjoy traditional sattvic breakfast, do some last-minute shopping for prasad and souvenirs, and head to the airport/station with memories of a lifetime.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about visiting Ayodhya.

❓

How do I book darshan at Ram Mandir?

+

You can visit Ram Mandir for darshan without any prior booking. The temple is open from 7:00 AM to 11:30 AM and 2:00 PM to 7:00 PM. For VIP darshan, you can book online through the official Shri Ram Janmabhoomi Teerth Kshetra trust website. It's recommended to visit early morning to avoid long queues, especially during festival seasons.

Is Ayodhya safe for solo female travelers?

+

Yes, Ayodhya is considered very safe for solo female travelers. The city has a strong security presence, especially around temple areas. Local people are warm, hospitable, and always willing to help. However, standard travel precautions should always be followed — stay in reputed accommodations, avoid isolated areas at night, and keep your valuables secure.

What is the best time to visit Ayodhya?

+

The best time to visit Ayodhya is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and cool (8°C–25°C). This period also coincides with major festivals like Deepotsav (October/November) and Ram Navami celebrations. Avoid visiting during peak summer (May-June) when temperatures can exceed 45°C.

How many days are enough for Ayodhya?

+

Ideally, 2-3 days are enough to cover the major attractions in Ayodhya. Day 1 can be dedicated to Ram Janmabhoomi and Hanuman Garhi, Day 2 for Kanak Bhawan, Dashrath Mahal, and other temples, and Day 3 for the ghats and remaining sites. If you want a leisurely experience, 4 days would be perfect.

Are there any food restrictions in Ayodhya?

+

Ayodhya is a vegetarian city, and non-vegetarian food and alcohol are not available within the city limits. All restaurants and eateries serve pure vegetarian, sattvic food. The city is famous for its pedas (sweetmeats), kachori-sabzi, and temple prasad. Embrace this as part of the spiritual experience!

Is there an airport in Ayodhya?

+

Yes! The Maharishi Valmiki International Airport (Ayodhya Dham) was inaugurated in 2024 and now handles direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Ahmedabad, and other major cities. The airport is located about 8 km from the city center and has modern facilities. Regular cab and auto services are available from the airport to the city.

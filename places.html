Holy Temples & Sacred Sites

Each temple and sacred site in Ayodhya carries the weight of millennia, inviting devotees to connect with the divine.

✦
Iconic Temple

Shri Ram Janmabhoomi

The majestic Ram Mandir stands at the birthplace of Lord Rama, constructed in the traditional Nagara style of temple architecture. Spanning 2.77 acres, this grand structure features three floors, each reaching 20 feet in height. The temple is adorned with 360 pillars, each intricately carved with depictions of gods and goddesses.

The sanctum sanctorum houses the idol of Ram Lalla (infant Lord Ram) in a divine ambiance that draws millions of devotees from across the world. The temple's pink sandstone construction, sourced from Rajasthan's Bansi-Paharpur, gives it a warm, celestial glow especially during sunrise and sunset.

📍 Central Ayodhya, near Sarayu River
🕐 7:00 AM – 11:30 AM & 2:00 PM – 7:00 PM
💰 Free Entry (VIP pass available)
⭐ Must-visit for every traveler

Hilltop Temple

Hanuman Garhi

Perched atop a hill in the heart of Ayodhya, Hanuman Garhi is a fortress-like temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman. Built in the 10th century, this sacred shrine is accessed by climbing 76 steps, with each step believed to wash away one's sins. The main deity is the image of Mother Anjani holding young Hanuman on her lap.

Legend has it that Lord Hanuman himself lives here to guard the city of his beloved Lord Ram. The temple offers breathtaking panoramic views of Ayodhya and the surrounding landscape, making it both a spiritual and visual treat.

📍 Naya Ghat Road, Ayodhya
🕐 5:00 AM – 10:00 PM
💰 Free Entry
🏔 76 Steps to the top

Royal Temple

Kanak Bhawan

Known as the "Golden Palace," Kanak Bhawan is one of Ayodhya's most beautifully adorned temples. According to tradition, it was gifted by Queen Kaikeyi to Goddess Sita at the time of her marriage with Lord Ram. The temple is famous for its golden-crowned idols of Lord Ram and Sita.

The current structure was rebuilt by Queen Vrisha Bhanu Kunwari of Tikamgarh in 1891. The temple's interior sparkles with gold and silver decorations, with the presiding deities wearing exquisite golden crowns studded with precious gems.

Ancient Shiva Temple

Nageshwarnath Temple

One of the oldest temples in Ayodhya, Nageshwarnath Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. According to legend, it was established by Kush, the son of Lord Rama. The temple is believed to be the only structure that survived the test of time when Ayodhya was lost to obscurity.

The temple holds special significance during the festival of Maha Shivaratri, when thousands of devotees gather for all-night prayers and celebrations. The ancient Shiva Linga enshrined here is said to have miraculous healing powers.

📍 Near Hanuman Garhi, Ayodhya
🕐 5:00 AM – 9:00 PM
💰 Free Entry
🔱 Established by Kush (son of Lord Ram)

Sacred Ghat

Guptar Ghat

Guptar Ghat is one of the most sacred and serene ghats on the banks of the Sarayu River. It is believed to be the place where Lord Rama took "Jal Samadhi" — his final departure from the mortal world. The ghat is surrounded by several temples and offers a peaceful atmosphere for meditation.

The beautifully maintained ghat features several small temples dedicated to various deities. During the evening aarti, the ghat transforms into a magical spectacle of lights, chants, and devotion that touches every visitor's soul.

📍 Banks of Sarayu River, Ayodhya
🕐 Open 24 hours
💰 Free
🌅 Best at sunset for aarti

Garden & Park

Tulsi Smarak Bhawan & Udyan

A beautiful memorial park dedicated to the great poet-saint Goswami Tulsidas, who composed the epic Ramcharitmanas. The sprawling gardens feature lush greenery, fountains, and a museum showcasing the life and works of Tulsidas.

The park serves as a tranquil retreat amidst the spiritual energy of Ayodhya. It's a perfect place for families, with walking paths, manicured lawns, and cultural exhibitions that bring the Ramayana to life through art and literature.

🕐 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Other Notable Sites

✦
Palace

Dashrath Mahal

The palace of King Dashrath, father of Lord Rama. Now converted into a temple with beautiful paintings depicting scenes from the Ramayana.

Temple

Treta Ke Thakur

An ancient temple believed to be the spot where Lord Rama performed the Ashwamedha Yagna. Features black-stone idols from Treta Yuga.

Heritage

Mani Parvat

A small hillock with Buddhist and Hindu significance. Said to be the spot where Hanuman dropped a piece of the Sanjeevani mountain.

Sacred

Swarg Dwar

The "Gateway to Heaven" — a sacred cremation ground where the last rites of King Dashrath are said to have been performed.

Ghat

Ram Ki Paidi

A stunning series of ghats on the Sarayu River, renovated with beautiful architecture. The main venue for the spectacular Deepotsav celebrations.

Heritage

Sita Ki Rasoi

Believed to be the kitchen of Goddess Sita. An ancient site showcasing traditional cooking utensils and the domestic life of the divine couple.
